## Proposal angle

Lance and Kim do not need HallPass/Anecdotal to become their day-to-day organic social media department. That would create the wrong kind of client relationship and too much recurring hand-holding.

The better move is a focused growth-readiness sprint:

- audit the current digital presence
- clarify what is working, what is confusing, and what is missing
- create a practical playbook Kim’s team can execute internally
- identify where paid ads could be worth testing
- clean up the website/tracking foundation so ads and social activity can actually be measured

The optional ongoing piece is paid ads management only — not organic social posting.

## What we heard

From the existing Lance/Kim/Nico Blue work and Ryan/Albert text exchange:

- Nico Blue already has some internal capacity for posting, but the team needs direction on what is worth posting and why.
- Existing website/tracking setup has gaps:
  - Google Analytics was not actively receiving website data during the setup call.
  - Shopify analytics and Google Ads data exist, but they are not a complete measurement foundation by themselves.
  - Google Search Console remains an open dependency for search visibility.
  - Local SEO/NAP consistency has already mattered because Google verification has been sensitive.
- The broader opportunity is not only Nico Blue. Lance wants the marketing foundation across his related businesses to become stronger over time.
- Albert is open to managing ads, but not organic social management.
- The BGC model is the right pattern: audit → strategy → playbook. For this client, expand the lens to include paid ads and website readiness.

## Recommended positioning

### Not this

“We’ll run your social media every day.”

That creates too much operational dependency and puts HallPass/Anecdotal in the middle of routine posts, approvals, and client preferences.

### This instead

“We’ll show your team exactly what to fix, what to keep doing, what to stop doing, and where paid ads could responsibly accelerate growth.”

This keeps the engagement strategic, useful, and bounded.

## Proposed engagement

# Digital Growth Audit + Playbook

A short, focused engagement to evaluate Nico Blue’s current social, website, local search, analytics, and paid ads foundation — then turn the findings into a practical playbook Kim’s team can use.

## Core deliverables

### 1. Social media audit

Review current social channels with emphasis on:

- platform role and audience fit
- what content currently feels strongest
- what content is likely being ignored
- use of Reels/video vs static posts
- posting patterns and missed opportunities
- visual consistency and brand trust
- local retail/boutique storytelling opportunities
- customer/community moments that should become content
- recommendations for what Kim’s team should keep, stop, and test

Important boundary: this is not a content calendar and not day-to-day posting management.

### 2. Paid ads readiness audit

Review whether paid ads are ready to scale or whether the foundation needs cleanup first:

- current Google Ads / Shopify connection status
- Meta ads account/page readiness, if applicable
- campaign objective recommendations
- audience and geography opportunities
- creative/message gaps
- landing page/shop path concerns
- tracking requirements before meaningful spend
- suggested first test campaign structure

Output should answer: “If we spend money here, what should we promote, to whom, and how will we know if it worked?”

### 3. Website + conversion audit

Review the website as the place social and paid traffic eventually lands:

- homepage clarity
- product/category path
- local store information and NAP consistency
- contact/location pages
- mobile experience
- Shopify analytics vs Google Analytics
- Search Console setup/readiness
- conversion friction
- trust signals
- local SEO basics for Southlake + Ardmore

Output should separate quick fixes from bigger improvements.

### 4. Measurement foundation

Define what should be tracked before the client spends more time or money on promotion:

- GA4 implementation status
- Search Console status
- Shopify analytics role
- Google Business Profile insights
- paid ads conversion tracking requirements
- simple monthly scorecard recommendations

The goal is not a complicated dashboard. The goal is knowing whether visibility, traffic, and revenue signals are improving.

### 5. Practical growth playbook

A concise, client-friendly playbook covering:

- what the audit found
- what matters most right now
- recommended social approach
- recommended paid ads approach
- website/tracking fixes
- first 30 days of action steps
- what Kim’s team can own internally
- what HallPass/Anecdotal could optionally manage later

Recommended format: hosted web playbook + PDF export.

## Out of scope

To keep this clean and prevent scope creep:

- no daily organic social media management
- no full content calendar
- no ongoing post-by-post approvals
- no unlimited website changes
- no ad spend included
- no full Shopify redesign
- no full SEO campaign beyond audit/recommendations unless separately scoped
