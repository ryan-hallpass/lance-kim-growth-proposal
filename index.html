02
The strategic call

This should not be positioned as organic social management.

That is the wrong shape for this client. The better offer is a focused growth-readiness sprint: evaluate what exists, show what to fix, give Kim’s team a playbook, and only offer ongoing management where it makes sense — paid ads.

Audit first

Social, website, paid ads, analytics, search, and local visibility reviewed as one connected system.

Playbook second

Clear recommendations Kim’s team can actually execute internally. No vague “post more” advice.

Ads only if ready

If the foundation is good enough, Albert can manage paid campaigns separately. Ad spend is not included.

03
What we heard

The opportunity is bigger than one channel.

Nico Blue has existing activity and internal capacity, but the system needs clarity: what content matters, how ads should be tested, whether tracking is trustworthy, and whether the website is ready to convert the attention being generated.

Social needs direction

Kim’s team can stay close to organic content, but they need a stronger filter for what is worth capturing and posting.

Tracking needs cleanup

GA4, Shopify analytics, Google Ads, Search Console, and Google Business Profile should be reconciled before paid spend is treated as scalable.

Website matters

Paid and organic traffic eventually hit the site. The store path, local info, mobile experience, and trust signals need to be part of the audit.

04
Proposed engagement

Digital Growth Audit + Playbook

A short engagement to evaluate the current foundation and deliver a clear set of recommendations across social, paid ads, website, tracking, and local search.

Social audit

Channel roles, content quality, video/Reels usage, visual consistency, missed storytelling opportunities, and internal execution guidance.

Paid ads readiness

Campaign objectives, account readiness, targeting, creative/message gaps, landing-path concerns, and first-test recommendations.

Website + measurement

Website conversion friction, GA4/GSC readiness, Shopify analytics role, GBP signals, and a simple scorecard for future decisions.
